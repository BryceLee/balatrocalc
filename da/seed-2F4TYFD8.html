Full route and notes

Pros: Round 1 Yorick is enough to carry the early antes. Most key vouchers show up early, especially Antimatter.

Cons: Spectral hunting can be rough. Sixth Sense and Seance can whiff into Red Seal or Mystery, and Negative tags can be streaky.

Route overview

  • Round 1: take Yorick from the Arcana pack, then discard aggressively. You can clear the first 10 antes with minimal extra power.
  • By ante 8: pick up Brainstorm, Mime, Baron, and Seance. If a voucher tag appears, take it; keep a Mystery card if you want a backup plan.
  • Midgame: Antimatter arrives early. Swap in Showman to find a second Blueprint. Triple-copy setup clears ante 16 smoothly.
  • Ante 16: lock a Troubadour with SL route (hand-size tech). No Turtle Bean here; expect +2 hand size.
  • Ante 24: you can lock Turtle Bean with SL route for +5 hand size.
  • Ante 25: Wild Negative stamp appears if you hold Showman or have no stamp. By then, economy is already stabilized.
  • Ante 30: small blind Negative tag; around 55 rerolls you can see DNA. SL can grab Negative DNA, but timing matters.
  • Negative tag requires strong power. With Turtle Bean, you should be able to clear big blind and secure the tag.
  • With the above setup and 2 Spectral hits, you can clear ante 32 without damage.
